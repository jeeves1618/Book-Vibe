Data Science for Business — 10-Week Study Planner Data Science for Business by Provost & Fawcett

Start date: 1 December 2025 · Duration: 10 weeks · Weekly milestones (one chapter per week). Link to book: O'Reilly, Amazon

About the book

Data Science for Business by Foster Provost and Tom Fawcett teaches data-analytic thinking and the principles behind data mining and predictive modeling — focusing on how to apply these techniques to real business problems.

Planner maps one chapter per week plus a final wrap-up week. Each week includes reading goals and practical exercises to translate the chapter concepts into hands-on work.

Week Dates Chapter (Reading) Focus / Learning Goals Practice / Deliverables Done
Week 1 Dec 1 — Dec 7, 2025 1. Introduction: Data‑Analytic Thinking Understand data‑analytic thinking, problem framing, CRISP‑DM style workflow.
  • Create a one‑page summary: what is data‑analytic thinking?
  • Find one business problem and frame it as a data problem.
Week 2 Dec 8 — Dec 14, 2025 2. Business Problems and Data Science Solutions Map business objectives to analytic tasks; cost/benefit thinking; evaluation metrics for business.
  • Pick the business problem from Week 1 and define success metrics.
  • Sketch a decision‑analytic flow (who acts on model output?).
Week 3 Dec 15 — Dec 21, 2025 3. Introduction to Predictive Modeling: From Correlation to Supervised Segmentation Learn supervised vs unsupervised, segmentation, predictive vs descriptive; basics of modeling.
  • Run a simple supervised model (e.g., logistic regression) on a small dataset.
  • Try a clustering algorithm and compare segmentation vs supervised labels.
Buffer Dec 22 — Dec 28, 2025 Review / Catch‑up Consolidate Weeks 1–3, finish any incomplete practice, refine problem framing.
  • Polish the Week 1–3 notebooks and write a short README.
  • If needed, add extra experiments (feature ideas, baseline models).
Week 4 Dec 29 — Jan 4, 2026 4. Fitting a Model to Data Model fitting principles, loss functions, training/validation splits, overfitting signals.
  • Implement training/validation split and measure performance.
  • Experiment with different loss functions for a regression/classification task.
Week 5 Jan 5 — Jan 11, 2026 5. Overfitting and Its Avoidance Bias‑variance tradeoff, regularization, cross‑validation, model selection strategies.
  • Run k‑fold cross‑validation and compare model complexity vs performance.
  • Apply regularization (L1/L2) and document effects.
Week 6 Jan 12 — Jan 18, 2026 6. Similarity, Neighbors, and Clusters Distance metrics, k‑NN, clustering algorithms, evaluating clusters.
  • Implement k‑NN classifier and measure sensitivity to K and scaling.
  • Run k‑means and hierarchical clustering; evaluate cluster quality (silhouette).
Buffer Jan 19 — Jan 25, 2026 Review / Project Consolidate Weeks 4–6, refine models, and prepare mid‑book mini project.
  • Create a mini project combining model fitting and cluster analysis on a single dataset.
  • Document choices and business implications.
Week 7 Jan 26 — Feb 1, 2026 7. Decision Analytic Thinking I: What Is a Good Model? Decision theory basics, utility, cost-sensitive decisions, aligning models to business value.
  • Compute expected value of model‑driven actions for your Week 1 problem.
  • Explore cost‑sensitive thresholds and choose an operating point.
Week 8 Feb 2 — Feb 8, 2026 8. Visualizing Model Performance ROC, precision–recall curves, lift charts, calibration — visual tools to evaluate models.
  • Plot ROC and PR curves for models built earlier; compute AUC and PR‑AUC.
  • Create calibration plots and lift charts; interpret results.
Week 9 Feb 9 — Feb 15, 2026 9. Evidence and Probabilities Thinking probabilistically, Bayesian vs frequentist views, likelihood ratios, evidence aggregation.
  • Work through a probability model and compute posterior/likelihood for a simple case.
  • Discuss how probabilistic outputs affect decision thresholds.
Week 10 Feb 16 — Feb 22, 2026 10. Representing and Mining Text Text representations (bag‑of‑words, TF‑IDF), tokenization, basic NLP for predictive tasks.
  • Build TF‑IDF features from a small corpus and train a classifier.
  • Try simple text preprocessing pipelines (stopwords, stemming/lemmatization).
Wrap‑up Feb 23 — Feb 29, 2026 Final Review & Project Consolidate all chapters; finalize a capstone project that demonstrates business value.
  • Finish a capstone notebook combining problem framing, modeling, evaluation, and decision analysis.
  • Prepare a 1‑page summary and deliverables for portfolio.
